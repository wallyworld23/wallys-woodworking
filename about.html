My Philosophy

Why I Build the Way I Build

A few things I believe about woodworking — and about the relationship between a craftsman and the people he builds for.

Wood Has Character

Every board has a story in its grain. I hand-select each piece of lumber for color, figure, and character — because two boards from the same species can look completely different, and the right one makes all the difference in the finished piece.

Built to Last Generations

I build with solid hardwoods and time-tested joinery methods. The goal isn't just a piece that looks good today — it's a piece that will outlast the house it's in. Mortise and tenon, dovetails, hand-cut joinery where it counts.

One Client at a Time

I take on a limited number of projects at a time. That means you get my full focus — not a production line. Every piece I build is a direct reflection of what you asked for, not a variation of whatever's easiest to manufacture in bulk.
